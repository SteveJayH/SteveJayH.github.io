Research

I'm intereted in optimization, computer vision, and microscopy. Much of my research is about acquiring and analyzing neuronal signal efficiently and accurately. Representative papers are highlighted.

* indicates co-first authors

3DM: Deep decomposition and deconvolution microscopy for rapid neural activity imaging
Eun-Seo Cho*, Seungjae Han*, Kang-Han Lee, Cheol-Hee Kim, Young-Gyu Yoon
Optics Express, 2021   (Editor's pick, Image of the Week)
paper / code

A computational microscopy for the volumetric imaging of neural activity, using two neural networks which perform sparse decomposition and deconvolution. Demonstrated via in vivo imaging of the neural activity of a whole larval zebrafish brain up to 4.2 VPS.

Efficient Neural Network Approximation of Robust PCA for Automated Analysis of Calcium Imaging Data
Seungjae Han, Eun-Seo Cho, Inkyu Park, Kijung Shin, Young-Gyu Yoon
MICCAI, 2021
paper / code

Fast and scalable gradient descent based Robust PCA algorithm. Also, it could be cascaded with downstream neural network and trained end-to-end. BEAR achieves an order of magnitude speed improvement to the baselines. Demonstrated with calcium imaging dataset as large as tens of gigabytes.

Smartphone Application to Estimate Distances from LTE Base Stations Based on Received Signal Strength Measurements
Seungjae Han, Taewon Kang, Jiwon Seo
ITC-CSCC, 2019   (Oral)
paper

Smartphone application that calculates predicted distance to near base stations based on received signal strength. Research for LTE-based positioning where GPS signal is unavailable.

Observation of Human Trajectory in Response to Haptic Feedback from Mobile Robot
Hee-Seung Moon, Woohyun Kim, Seungjae Han, Jiwon Seo
ICCAS, 2018
paper

Analyze the walking trajectory of a blindfolded participant receiving hapic feedback from a mobile robot. Developed a mobile robot system with a haptic device and algorithm for appropriate haptic feedback.
